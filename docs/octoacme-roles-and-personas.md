# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals validate that software meets quality standards, acceptance criteria, and user expectations. They design test strategies, execute testing, and ensure issues are identified and resolved before release.

### Responsibilities
- Create comprehensive test plans and test cases
- Execute manual and automated testing
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Report and track defects with clear reproduction steps
- Collaborate with developers on testability and bug fixes
- Conduct regression, integration, and user acceptance testing

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal production defects
- Reduce testing cycle time through automation
- Improve test coverage across critical user flows
- Provide fast, actionable feedback to developers

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report test status and blockers
- Bug reports and test results in issue tracking systems
- Test plan reviews with product and engineering teams
- Post-release validation reports

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers:** Collaborate on testability, review acceptance criteria, and verify bug fixes
- **Product Manager:** Validate that features meet acceptance criteria and user needs
- **Project Manager:** Report test progress, blockers, and release readiness
- **UX Designer:** Ensure UI/UX matches design specifications

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own the user experience from research through design implementation. They ensure that products are usable, accessible, and delightful by conducting user research, creating design mockups, and collaborating with Product Managers and Developers.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability studies
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user flows and information architecture
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) are met
- Collaborate on design systems and component libraries
- Validate designs through user testing and iteration
- Partner with developers during implementation to ensure design fidelity

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Increase user satisfaction and engagement metrics
- Maintain design consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product and engineering teams
- User research findings and recommendations
- Design specifications and handoff documentation
- Feedback sessions on implemented features

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager:** Align on user needs, product vision, and feature priorities
- **Developers:** Collaborate on design feasibility, implementation, and design refinement
- **QA/Testing:** Ensure designs are properly implemented and accessible
- **Data Analyst:** Use metrics to inform design decisions and measure impact
- **Stakeholders:** Present design rationale and incorporate feedback

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts support data-driven decision making by measuring success metrics, analyzing trends, and providing actionable insights. They work closely with Product Managers to define KPIs, build dashboards, and inform product strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Build and maintain dashboards and reports
- Analyze user behavior and feature adoption
- Conduct A/B test analysis and experimentation
- Identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities in data
- Translate business questions into data queries and insights
- Ensure data quality and accuracy

### Goals
- Enable data-informed product decisions
- Increase visibility into product health and user behavior
- Reduce time to insights through automated reporting
- Measure impact of features and improvements

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews with product and leadership
- Data insights presentations and recommendations
- Dashboard documentation and self-service guides
- Ad-hoc analysis requests and findings

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager:** Define success metrics, inform roadmap decisions, measure feature impact
- **Developers:** Collaborate on instrumentation and data collection
- **UX Designer:** Provide user behavior insights to inform design
- **Project Manager:** Track project health metrics and report progress
- **Stakeholders:** Present business impact and ROI analysis

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Lead

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (or Delivery Leads) facilitate agile practices, remove impediments, and coach teams on iterative delivery. They ensure the team follows agreed-upon processes, continuously improves, and delivers value efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and process impediments
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Track team velocity and iteration health
- Protect the team from context switching and scope creep
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Coordinate with Project Managers on cross-team dependencies

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and sustainable pace
- Reduce cycle time and delivery friction
- Improve team collaboration and communication
- Ensure predictable, high-quality delivery

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and team syncs
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation
- Process improvement proposals and updates
- Escalation of blockers to Project Managers or leadership

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers:** Remove blockers, facilitate collaboration, coach on best practices
- **Project Manager:** Coordinate on schedules, dependencies, and resource needs
- **Product Manager:** Ensure backlog is ready for sprint planning
- **QA/Testing:** Coordinate testing activities within sprint cycle
- **All Team Members:** Facilitate retrospectives and implement improvements

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders provide business context, strategic direction, and approval authority for projects. Different stakeholder types have varying levels of involvement and influence.

### Stakeholder Types

#### Business Sponsor
**Role:** Provides funding, strategic direction, and executive approval  
**Responsibilities:**
- Define business objectives and ROI expectations
- Approve project scope, budget, and resources
- Make go/no-go decisions at key milestones
- Remove organizational blockers

**Engagement Pattern:** Monthly updates, milestone reviews, escalated decisions

#### Support Team
**Role:** Will support the product post-launch and represents support needs  
**Responsibilities:**
- Provide input on common customer issues and pain points
- Review and validate support documentation
- Test customer-facing features for clarity
- Provide feedback on supportability

**Engagement Pattern:** Planning phase input, pre-release reviews, post-launch feedback

#### Sales Team
**Role:** Represents customer needs and market requirements  
**Responsibilities:**
- Share customer feedback and competitive insights
- Validate feature value propositions
- Provide input on go-to-market timing
- Support beta customer engagement

**Engagement Pattern:** Feature planning input, demo participation, launch coordination

#### End Users / Customers
**Role:** Ultimate beneficiaries of the product  
**Responsibilities:**
- Participate in user research and testing
- Provide feedback on usability and value
- Validate that solutions solve real problems

**Engagement Pattern:** User research sessions, beta testing, feedback surveys

### General Stakeholder Communication
- Project kickoff and milestone reviews
- Regular status updates (cadence varies by stakeholder type)
- Change requests and scope discussions
- Post-launch impact reviews

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager:** Define requirements, validate solutions, measure success
- **Project Manager:** Receive status updates, approve changes, resolve escalations
- **UX Designer:** Participate in user research, provide feedback on designs
- **All Roles:** Provide business context and strategic direction
